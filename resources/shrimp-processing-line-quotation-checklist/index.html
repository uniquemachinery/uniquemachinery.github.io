Shrimp Processing Line Quotation Checklist for Seafood Factories

A useful shrimp processing line quotation starts with shrimp size, peeling style, deveining requirement, capacity, water supply, drainage and packing preparation details.

Quotation Checklist8 min readUpdated 2026
Direct answer: Seafood factories should prepare product specifications before asking for shrimp peeling machine or shrimp peeling and deveining line prices. The supplier needs to know shrimp size range, shell condition, raw or cooked status, whether deveining is required, target hourly output, water and drainage conditions, and whether the line connects to washing, inspection, freezing or packing preparation.

Who this guide is for

This guide is for frozen shrimp factories, seafood processors, ready-to-cook seafood suppliers, central kitchens and import buyers evaluating industrial seafood processing equipment. It is especially useful when the buyer wants more than a single machine price and needs a practical process recommendation.

Typical production needs

Shrimp processing lines usually focus on stable peeling quality, hygiene, water management and easy inspection. Some buyers need a shrimp peeling machine only. Others need feeding, peeling, deveining, washing, sorting, drainage and packing preparation combined into a line.

What to prepare before quotation

  • Shrimp species or product photos and size range.
  • Raw, cooked, frozen or thawed condition before processing.
  • Peeling style and whether deveining is required.
  • Hourly capacity target and number of working shifts.
  • Water supply, drainage, floor slope and cleaning plan.
  • Factory layout, voltage and downstream packing or freezing process.

Common mistakes to avoid

A common mistake is asking for one machine price without confirming shrimp size and product specification. Another mistake is ignoring water and drainage. Shrimp processing is a wet process, so drainage, cleaning access and operator inspection space affect practical operation. Buyers should also avoid assuming that one configuration fits every shrimp product. Capacity and peeling performance depend on material condition and processing goal.

Common buyer questions

What information should I send first?

Send shrimp photos, size range, raw or cooked condition, peeling and deveining target, capacity and factory utility information.

Can a line be customized?

Line configuration can be reviewed according to raw material, process requirement, layout and final product specification.

Should I ask for a video?

A machine video can help buyers understand operation and material flow, but quotation still requires raw material and capacity details.
